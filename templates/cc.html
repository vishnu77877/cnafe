Cadang Cadang

Introduction

Cadang-cadang is a disease caused by Coconut cadang-cadang viroid (CCCVd), a lethal viroid of coconut (Cocos nucifera), anahaw (Saribus rotundifolius) buri (Corypha utan), and African oil palm (Elaeis guineensis). The name cadang-cadang comes from the word gadang-gadang that means dying in Bicol.

Symptoms

  • 1-2 years after infection; no leaf symptoms, but newly developing nuts become more rounded and show equatorial scarifications.
  • Chlorotic, non-necrotic, 'water-soaked' leaf spots appear; more nuts rounded and scarified; inflorescence stunted with necrotic tips, loss of some male florets.
  • Leaf spots enlarge; fewer nuts produced, new inflorescence stunted and sterile; fibrous tissue remains attached to petioles as 'winging'.
  • Production of spathes, inflorescence and nuts declines, then ceases; numerous leaf spots.
  • Fronds decline in size and number and leaflets become brittle; leaf spots coalesce to give general chlorotic appearance; crown size reduced; stem tapering; death of palm.
  • Symptoms can resemble physiological changes due to poor nutrition and water stress in the field, typhoon damage, and sterility.
  • Leaf spots resemble insect feeding damage and microbial infection.

Control Measures and Nutrient recommendation

  • Exclusion is the only method considered to be effective in controlling the spread of CCCVd.
  • Cut down and burn affected trees; do this as soon as the disease is seen.
  • Certification of origin from a disease-free area is the principal method currently used
